About AKGEC

Scaling the Zenith of Excellence, Ajay Kumar Garg Engineering College (AKGEC) has established itself as one of the leading technical institutions in Uttar Pradesh.

Established in 1998, Ajay Kumar Garg Engineering College (AKGEC) is affiliated with Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University, Lucknow. The college is renowned for offering high-quality B.Tech and M.Tech programs in various engineering disciplines, including Computer Science, Electronics, Mechanical, and Civil Engineering.

AKGEC has been consistently recognized for its academic excellence, boasting top-notch university rankings and industry placements. With an emphasis on research and development, the college has set up specialized centers of competence and collaboration with leading global companies, such as BOSCH Rexroth AG and SIEMENS.

The college prides itself on its industry-oriented education, promoting a blend of theoretical learning and hands-on training to equip students with the skills required to succeed in the ever-evolving technological landscape.

AKGEC Conference

The objective of the conference is to exchange new knowledge and recent developments in computational techniques, mathematical modelling, energy systems, and more. It aims to provide a forum for engineers, researchers, and specialists worldwide to discuss applied sciences in emerging technology and computational intelligence for sustainable technologies.

The International Conference on Relevance of Applied Sciences to Emerging Technology (ROASET-II) is organized by the Department of Applied Sciences & Humanities, Ajay Kumar Garg Engineering College, Ghaziabad (UP), affiliated with Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University, Lucknow (UP), India.

The conference will provide an opportunity for researchers, academicians, and students to share their knowledge and discuss the latest developments in their field of expertise. This platform enables young researchers to interact with eminent scientists and researchers globally. Equal opportunities are provided for all participants to share their ideas in interdisciplinary fields such as mathematics, statistics, computational intelligence, and renewable energy.

About Ghaziabad

Ghaziabad City Overview

Ghaziabad is a city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh and a part of the NCR. It is the administrative headquarters of Ghaziabad district and the largest city in western Uttar Pradesh, with a population of 1,729,000. Well connected by roads and railways, it is a major rail junction for North India. Known as the "Saya of Uttar Pradesh" due to its proximity to New Delhi, it is one of the fastest-growing cities in the world.

The city was founded in 1740 A.D. by Ghazi-ud-Din, and named "Ghaziuddinnagar." It became known as "Ghaziabad" with the opening of the Railways in 1864. The city has two major divisions separated by the Hindon River: Trans-Hindon and Cis-Hindon. Ghaziabad played a significant role during the Mughal period and became a key railway junction for the region.

Ghaziabad’s development saw a significant milestone with the establishment of the Scientific Society and the opening of the Sind, Punjab & Delhi Railway line. This development made the city a major rail junction, connecting Delhi and Multan through Ghaziabad.

About Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh Overview

Uttar Pradesh ('Northern Province') is the most populous state in India, with over 200 million inhabitants. It was created in 1937 as the United Provinces of Agra and Oudh during British rule and renamed to Uttar Pradesh in 1950. It is bordered by Rajasthan, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Delhi, Uttarakhand, Nepal, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Jharkhand, and Chhattisgarh.

The state is divided into 18 divisions and 75 districts, with the state capital being Lucknow. The major rivers of the state, the Ganges and Yamuna, join at Triveni Sangam in Prayagraj. The state is also known for its agricultural output, with 82% of its area being cultivable.

Economy and Tourism

Uttar Pradesh has a growing economy dominated by the services industry, including tourism, real estate, and financial consultancies. It is the second-largest state economy in India, with a GDP of ₹21.73 lakh crore (US$290 billion).

Uttar Pradesh ranks first in domestic tourist arrivals in India, with over 71 million visitors. The state is home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri. Its tourism sector is growing at 21.60% annually.
